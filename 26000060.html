What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, networks, mobile devices, and personal information from digital threats. These threats can come in the form of hackers, viruses, or even fake websites trying to steal your data. In today‚Äôs digital world, almost everything ‚Äî from school assignments to bank accounts ‚Äî is connected to the internet. This makes cybersecurity more important than ever.

Common Cyber Threats

There are many types of cyber threats, and they can affect anyone ‚Äî students, businesses, schools, and even governments. Understanding them helps us stay safe online.

  • Phishing: This is when you receive a fake email or message that looks real, asking you to click a link or share personal details like your password. These messages often trick people into giving away sensitive information.
  • Viruses & Malware: These are harmful programs that can infect your device, slow it down, delete files, or even spy on you. They usually come from suspicious downloads or fake websites.
  • Ransomware: A serious type of malware that locks your files and demands payment (a ransom) to unlock them. Even hospitals and schools have been targeted by this.
  • Data Breaches: When hackers break into databases and steal large amounts of information like names, emails, or credit card numbers. This can lead to identity theft or fraud.

Example: In 2021, a large US fuel company called Colonial Pipeline was attacked by ransomware. The hackers shut down fuel supplies and demanded millions of dollars. This affected gas stations and caused panic buying across the country. It shows how powerful cyber threats can be.
