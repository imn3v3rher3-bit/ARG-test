Protect Your Account

Having a strong password, helps to defend accounts against data threat such as Brute Force Attacks and Thwarts Dictionary Attacks. Because of it being generally sophicated, it prevents the reuse of passwords on other sites. Such that, in the event that an account gets hacked by Social Engineering Shortcuts, it would be less likely for other accounts to get hacked.


TIP: Do not include birthday, famous dates, increasingly consecutive numbers and common words in your password.



What is Multi-Factor Authenication?

Multi-Factor Authenication uses more than one form of authenication to ensure that the person logging in to the account is authenic.




Examples of Multi-Factor Authenication:

  1. Your Password

  2. Hardware Key

  3. Code Generator

  4. Fingerprint

  5. Composition Of Face

  6. Biometric Data




Importance of Multi-Factor Authenication:

  • Adds Extra Layers of Security

  • Reduces Risks of Account Takeover

  • Protects Sensitive Information

  • Builds Trust



Adds Extra Layers of Security

Having an more than one source of authentication creates at least ONE extra layer of security in the event that the password is compromised. With high amounts of security put in place, more people are able to put their trust in the system and will be more incline to put their sensitive information on the platform.

However, there is still the likelihood of hackers being able to bypass the authentication, so PROCCEED WITH CAUTION!
