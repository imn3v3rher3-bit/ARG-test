Common Types of Cyber-Attacks


Malware

Malware definition:
Malware is short for malicious software. It refers to any software or code designed to harm, exploit,
or illegally access computers, networks, or devices. Some of which includes:
  1. Virus - Attach to files and spread when the files are shared
  2. Worms - Spread on their own through networks without needing to attach to files
  3. Trojans - Disguise themselves as normal software to trick users into installing them

Example of Malware:

You download a free game from a sketchy site, and your computer starts acting weird.
A hidden trojan is stealing your data or giving hackers access.

How to avoid malware:
  • Install and update antivirus software.
  • Keep your system and apps updated.
  • Avoid clicking unknown links or downloading suspicious files.
  • Back up important data regularly.


Phishing

Phishing definition:
Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick people into giving personal information, like passwords
or credit card numbers, by pretending to be a trusted source, often through fake emails or websites.


Example of Phishing:

You might get an email that looks like it’s from your bank, asking you to “verify your account” by clicking a link.
That link leads to a fake website designed to steal your login details.



How to protect yourself from Phishing:
  • Don’t click on suspicious links or attachments
  • Check email addresses and URLs carefully
  • Never share sensitive info through email or text
  • Use two-factor authentication for extra security


Ransomware

Ransomware definition:
Ransomware is a type of malware that locks or encrypts your files, making them unusable, and then demands
money to unlock them. It often spreads through infected email attachments, fake downloads, or unsafe websites.


Example of Ransomware

You open a suspicious email attachment, and suddenly your screen shows a message saying your files are locked.
It then asks you to pay money—often in cryptocurrency—to get them back.


How prevent it:
  • Keep backups of your important files
  • Use antivirus and keep your system updated
  • Don’t open suspicious emails or links
  • Avoid downloading software from untrusted sources
