Safe Practices On Social Media Use

Protect Your Personal Information

    Avoid sharing sensitive data like your home address, phone number, full birthdate, or financial details.
    Think twice before posting photos that reveal personal locations or identificaton numbers.
    Regularly review and update your privacy settings to control who can see your content and personal data.

Be Mindful of What You Post

    Once something is online, it can be copied, shared, or misused-even if you delete it.
    Avoid posting content that could harm your reputation or someone else's.
    Consider how your words and images might be interpreted by different audiences.

Use Strong and Unique Passwords

    Use complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
    Avoid using the same password across multiple platforms.
    Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possibles.
What is Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is a security process that requires two different forms of verification to access an account. After entering your password, you must confirm your identity using a second methodâ€”such as a code sent to your phone, an authentication app, or a fingerprint scan. This extra step helps protect your account even if your password is stolen.

Watch out for Scams and Phishing

    Be cautious with messages or links from unknown sources.
    Report suspicious activity to the social media provider immediately.

Be critical of What You See and Share

    Verify information before reposting - it might be false or misleading.
    Follow credible news sources and fact-check suspicious claims.
UNDERSTAND THAT NOT EVERYTHING ON SOCIAL MEDIA REFLECTS REALITY.
